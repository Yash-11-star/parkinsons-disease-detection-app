Welcome to Parkinson's Disease Detector


Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement control. It is characterized by the loss of dopamine-producing neurons in the brain, leading to symptoms such as tremors, stiffness, and impaired balance. While the exact cause is unknown, both genetic and environmental factors are believed to contribute. Parkinson's primarily affects older individuals, and its symptoms gradually worsen over time. Although there is no cure, various treatments, including medication and therapy, can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life for those affected.

ABOUT

The anticipated outcome of this innovative Parkinson's disease detection project is a robust and reliable system capable of accurately diagnosing individuals based on a dual-modal analysis of spiral and wave drawings and recorded speech. By harnessing machine learning algorithms, the system aims to discern subtle patterns indicative of Parkinson's disease, offering healthcare professionals a comprehensive tool for early and nuanced detection. The successful implementation of this system holds the potential to revolutionize Parkinson's disease diagnosis by addressing the limitations of existing methods. The dual-model approach, encompassing both drawing and speech analysis, is expected to significantly enhance diagnostic accuracy, allowing for more timely interventions and personalized treatment plans.
